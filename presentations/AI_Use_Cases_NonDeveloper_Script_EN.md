## [#3] How I Used AI Before

**Key flow:**
- Late 2022 — ChatGPT launched → Shifted from Googling everything to asking ChatGPT and verifying the answer → Started solving dev-adjacent tasks myself (Slack bots via AppScript, Excel automation, etc.)
- As more models emerged (Gemini, Perplexity, Grok) → Learned to leverage each model's strengths:
  - **Perplexity + Grok** — research & fact-finding
  - **Gemini** — strategy and workflow planning
  - **ChatGPT** — English translation and polishing copy
  - **Gemini + Perplexity + Grok** — triple-checking results

**Key takeaways:**
- Maximise each model's strength, use only what you need → minimise paid subscriptions
- Beyond replacing coding tasks, I started thinking more about **user experience**
- Shifted from "building things for myself" → "building things others can easily use"

## [#7] NotebookLM

1. An online research and note-taking tool developed by Google Labs
2. **Core feature: No hallucinations** — answers are grounded strictly in the documents you provide
3. Google-based → accessible directly through Gemini
4. Upload any supported document type into one project, then query across all of it

**Use case: Internal policy bot**
- Export Sentree (Notion) + Bebot database content as `.md` files
- Upload to NotebookLM
- Build a Gemini Gem connected to the notebook → anyone can ask Gemini questions about internal policies at any time
- HR team updates and maintenance handled via Claude

**Wider potential:**
- Product onboarding, user guides, knowledge bases → natural language answers from your own data

## [#8-1] Wrap-up — Limitations

AI is not a magic wand:

1. Clicking a button doesn't make you an expert. Technical judgment still requires human thought.
2. AI output still needs human decision-making. Review and iteration are non-negotiable.
3. The initial setup takes time. You need dedicated personal time to configure AI for your workflows.
4. Working *with* AI adds a new communication layer. There's a learning curve — for you and your team.
5. **Most importantly: Security.** We handle personal data. Using AI responsibly is an added responsibility we must take seriously.

---

## [#8-2] Wrap-up — The Upside

That said, the personal and professional growth is real:

1. You pick up what you need as you go — learning happens organically through doing
2. Repeated decision-making on AI output moves you from task-executor to higher-level thinker
3. You naturally develop cross-functional understanding; technical communication with other teams gets easier
4. AI communication time and costs decrease over time — once configured, AI remembers the context
5. With data training disabled and clear guardrails in place → dramatic speed improvements are achievable
   - If we collectively address the personal data challenge, SentBe can build a truly effective AI-ready environment very soon
