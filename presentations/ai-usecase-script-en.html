#3
How I Used AI Before

Late 2022 — ChatGPT launched and I immediately shifted from Googling everything to asking ChatGPT and verifying the answer. That alone unlocked things I couldn't do before: Slack bots via AppScript, Excel automation, small scripts that used to require a developer.

As more models emerged — Gemini, Perplexity, Grok — I started learning to use each one for what it does best, rather than paying for a single subscription:

  • Perplexity + Grok — real-time research and fact-checking
  • Gemini — strategy planning and workflow mapping
  • ChatGPT — English translation and copy polishing
  • Gemini + Perplexity + Grok — triple-checking outputs for accuracy
Key Insight
Maximising each model's strength and only paying for what you actually need is a strategy in itself. It also pushed me to think beyond just "doing tasks faster" — I started thinking about user experience: not just building things for myself, but building things that others can actually use.

#5
Gemini 3.0
Recent AI Usage

Gemini 3.0 was a significant leap. The deeper Google Workspace integration now means direct control over Docs, Sheets, and Drive — which makes it much more useful for actual work, not just chat.

Biggest Advantage for SentBe
Gemini Pro is now included in our existing Google Workspace paid plan. That makes it the only AI tool currently available company-wide — zero additional cost.

Beyond the basics, we're using the Gem feature to build lightweight custom AI assistants:

  • Benefits receipt review
  • IT asset request document assistance
  • Internal document review support

Gemini also connects directly to NotebookLM — which I'll explain in a moment.

#7
NotebookLM
Recent AI Usage

NotebookLM is an online research and note-taking tool developed by Google Labs. It stands apart from other AI tools for one key reason:

"No hallucinations — answers are grounded strictly in the documents you provide. If your source is accurate, the answer will be too."

Because it's Google-based, it's also accessible directly through Gemini, which makes the workflow seamless.

Use Case — Internal Policy Bot
  1. Export Sentree (Notion) + Bebot database content as .md files
  2. Upload to NotebookLM
  3. Build a Gemini Gem connected to the notebook
  4. Deploy → anyone can ask Gemini questions about internal policies at any time

HR team updates and maintenance handled via Claude.

The same pattern applies beyond HR: product onboarding, user guides, knowledge bases — anything you want to make queryable in natural language.

#8-1
Limitations
Wrap-up

AI is genuinely useful — but it's not a magic wand:

  1. Clicking a button doesn't make you an expert. Technical judgment still requires human thought and experience.
  2. AI output still needs human decision-making. Review and iteration are non-negotiable.
  3. The initial setup takes real time. You need dedicated personal time to configure AI for your workflows before it becomes useful.
  4. AI adds a new communication layer. Working with AI effectively requires a learning curve — for you and your team.
  5. Security is the most critical issue. We handle personal data. Using AI responsibly — knowing what goes in and where it goes — is a responsibility we must take seriously.
#8-2
The Upside
Wrap-up

That said, the growth is real — and it compounds:

  1. You learn what you need, as you need it. Capability grows organically through doing.
  2. Repeated decision-making on AI output elevates your role. You shift from task-executor to reviewer, director, and decision-maker.
  3. Cross-functional understanding follows naturally. Technical communication with other teams becomes easier over time.
  4. Costs and friction decrease as you go. Once configured, AI remembers the context. Time and credit costs drop steadily.
  5. With the right guardrails, the speed gains are dramatic. Turn off data training. Stay within defined boundaries. If we address the personal data challenge together, SentBe can build a truly effective AI-ready environment — sooner than we think.
#8-3
The Key Question
Wrap-up
"The most important thing is still Customer Centric."

Making things has gotten much easier — but usefulness still has to come first. No matter how fast you can build, it only matters if users actually need it.

Marketing sensibility, product thinking, and UX consideration remain essential. AI doesn't replace that judgment — it just removes the bottlenecks that used to slow it down.

#8-4
Closing
AI isn't here to replace people.
It's here to help people work faster and more easily.

I still have a lot to learn — and AI is evolving even faster than I can keep up.

But here's what I've found: one small win is all it takes to build the confidence that you can do this.

You don't need to follow every new development. Pick what's useful, use it within defined boundaries, and I believe everyone can find real value in AI.
